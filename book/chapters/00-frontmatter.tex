\chapter*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Preface}

The field of Generative AI is moving fast, and IBM's \textbf{watsonx} platform is where many enterprises will deploy it. This study guide is the companion text for the YouTube and Udemy series of the same name --- all three formats follow the official \textbf{IBM watsonx Generative AI Engineer v1 -- Associate (C1000-185)} exam blueprint.

\section*{Purpose of This Book}

This book is a structured, exam-oriented learning path covering Generative AI concepts, prompt engineering, fine-tuning, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), deployment, and orchestration on the IBM watsonx platform. Every chapter is mapped 1:1 to a section of the official exam objectives, and every code sample uses the \textbf{current watsonx.ai model catalogue and SDKs} (Granite 3.x, Llama 3.x, Mistral, Granite Guardian, Granite-Embedding, Slate v2, \texttt{ibm-watsonx-ai} Python SDK). Older identifiers such as \texttt{granite-13b-instruct-v2}, \texttt{llama-2-*}, and \texttt{flan-*} are flagged where they appear so you can recognise them on the exam without using them in new work.

\section*{Who This Book Is For}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{AI Engineers and Developers} validating their skills with IBM watsonx
    \item \textbf{Data Scientists} expanding into generative AI implementation
    \item \textbf{Solution Architects} designing AI-powered enterprise solutions
    \item \textbf{Technical Professionals} seeking IBM certification credentials
    \item \textbf{Self-Learners} building expertise in enterprise AI platforms
\end{itemize}
